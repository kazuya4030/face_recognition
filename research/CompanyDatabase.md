## Classic Venture Studios

### Idealab (Bill Gross, Pasadena)

- **Overview**: Widely cited as the original/longest-running startup studio/incubator. Classic incubator/company-builder model: originates ideas in-house, recruits founding teams, provides seed capital/office/shared services, spins companies out with Idealab retaining a minority stake. [rossdawson.com](https://rossdawson.com/startup-studios/bill-gross-idealab-company-builder-successful/) [marketplace.org](https://www.marketplace.org/story/2008/02/21/idealab-corporate-history)
- **History**: Founded 1996, Pasadena CA, by Bill Gross, modeled on Edison's invention lab. First fund ~$12M (1997), seeded by a $5M stake from ~10 investors incl. Steven Spielberg, Michael Douglas, Compaq chairman Ben Rosen. Incubator arm rebranded "Idealab Studio" in 2018, Gross as Chairman. [rossdawson.com](https://rossdawson.com/startup-studios/bill-gross-idealab-company-builder-successful/) [labusinessjournal.com](https://labusinessjournal.com/la500-2025/technology-2025/la500-2025-bill-gross/)
- **Founders**: Bill Gross — previously founded GNP Loudspeakers and Knowledge Adventure (sold to CUC International 1996, ~$100M, funding Idealab's founding capital). [grokipedia.com](https://grokipedia.com/page/Bill_T._Gross)
- **Funding**: Multiple internal funds (Crunchbase lists 3, incl. "Idealab Angels I"); aggregate totals not publicly disclosed. Confirmed: ~$12M first fund (1997). [crunchbase.com](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/idealab)
- **Companies launched**: >5,000 ideas evaluated over 25 years → ~150 companies founded (per Gross's own "25 lessons" retrospective). Tracxn (lower-confidence) counts ~127 supported startups. Gross has said he "advised and helped fund more than 100 companies." [x.com/businessbarista](https://x.com/businessbarista/status/1750243089602531460) [tracxn.com](https://tracxn.com/d/companies/idealab) [review.firstround.com](https://review.firstround.com/lessons-learned-from-bill-gross-35-ipos-and-40-failures/)
- **Exits**: Sources converge on ~35–50 IPOs/acquisitions (First Round Review: "35 successful IPOs and acquisitions"; other retrospectives round to "50"). Notable: **Overture/GoTo.com** — pioneered pay-per-click search ads; acquired by Yahoo! 2003 for $1.63B. **Picasa** sold to Google. **NetZero**, **CitySearch**, **Commission Junction** also notable. [rossdawson.com](https://rossdawson.com/startup-studios/bill-gross-idealab-company-builder-successful/) [marketplace.org](https://www.marketplace.org/story/2008/02/21/idealab-corporate-history)
- **Success rate**: Gross has publicly cited "35 IPOs/exits and 40 failures" — implying roughly ~45–50% success rate among ventures reaching a definitive outcome (total company count larger; many still private/unresolved). [review.firstround.com](https://review.firstround.com/lessons-learned-from-bill-gross-35-ipos-and-40-failures/)
- **Notable failures**: **eToys** — reached $10B peak market value, collapsed/filed bankruptcy after canceling IPO plans post dot-com crash. **Eve.com** — cosmetics e-commerce, launched June 1999, shut down October 2000, 164 jobs lost. [forbes.com](https://www.forbes.com/forbes/2002/0304/044a.html) [review.firstround.com](https://review.firstround.com/lessons-learned-from-bill-gross-35-ipos-and-40-failures/)
- **Profit structure**: Starts ventures at 100% ownership, immediately transfers ≥50% to founding team; provides initial capital (historically ~6 months runway); takes minority equity stake, helps raise outside capital ASAP. [softtechventures.com](https://www.softtechventures.com/post/from-past-to-present-venture-building-model)
- **Moat**: First-mover/"original venture studio" brand; 25+ years pattern-recognition and repeat-founder relationships; Gross's personal reputation/network. [artcenter.edu](https://www.artcenter.edu/about/get-to-know-artcenter/people/bill-gross.html)
- **Hiring**: Historically generates ideas internally and recruits CEOs/founding teams via Gross's network rather than an open EIR-application funnel; structured program details **unclear/not fully disclosed**.
- **Org structure**: Idealab Studio operates under Gross as Chairman; has spun out its own AI ventures directly. [labusinessjournal.com](https://labusinessjournal.com/la500-2025/technology-2025/la500-2025-bill-gross/)
- **Investment criteria**: **Not fully disclosed — unclear**. Gross has historically emphasized large, timing-sensitive markets and cheap/fast idea testing (per his well-known TED talk citing "timing" as the top correlate of startup success).
- **AI usage (2023–2026)**: Idealab Studio directly incubated **ProRata.ai**; Sept 2025 ProRata raised a $40M Series B and launched **Gist.ai** (AI search engine on licensed publisher content; partners incl. Time, Universal Music Group, News Media Alliance [Mar 2025], Boston Globe, Vox Media, Future [Jun 2025]). Bill Gross is CEO of ProRata/Gist. [labusinessjournal.com](https://labusinessjournal.com/la500-2025/technology-2025/la500-2025-bill-gross/) [idealab.com](https://www.idealab.com/bio/management/bill_gross.php)

### Betaworks (NYC)

- **Overview**: NYC hybrid startup studio/accelerator/venture fund. Builds companies internally, runs cohort incubator "Camp," and makes external seed/Series A investments. [builtinnyc.com](https://www.builtinnyc.com/articles/rise-startup-studios-new-york-city) [en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Betaworks)
- **History**: Founded 2007, NYC Meatpacking District, by John Borthwick and Andy Weissman (Weissman departed 2011 for Union Square Ventures). "Camp" 12-week cohort program launched 2016; WeWork invested in a physical "Betaworks Studios" space March 2019. [recreations.media](https://www.recreations.media/p/betaworks-trilogy-part-i-a-builders) [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2019/03/28/wework-backs-new-york-tech-clubhouse-betaworks-studios/)
- **Founders**: John Borthwick (CEO) — ex-CTO/SVP Technology at Time Warner, ex-CEO of Fotolog. Andy Weissman (co-founder, departed 2011). [en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Betaworks)
- **Funding**: Fund I $48M (2016), Fund II $46M (2020), Fund III $66M (closed July 2025, early-stage AI focus). Separate "Betaworks Ventures" $50M seed/Series A vehicle (2017) invested in 150+ companies. Typical check $250K–$500K; Fund III plans ≥25 pre-seed/seed investments + ≥50 via Camp. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2025/07/22/betaworks-third-fund-closes-at-66m-to-invest-in-early-stage-ai-startups/) [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2017/08/10/betaworks-ventures-is-a-not-so-new-50-million-early-stage-fund/)
- **Companies launched (internal)**: Giphy (→Meta), Dots (→Take-Two Interactive), Bitly (→PE buyer), Tweetdeck (→Twitter), Chartbeat, SocialFlow, Digg, Instapaper. [builtinnyc.com](https://www.builtinnyc.com/articles/rise-startup-studios-new-york-city)
- **Exits**: As investor (not builder): Twitter, Tumblr, Kickstarter, Medium, Anchor, Gimlet Media, plus recent AI bets Hugging Face, The Browser Company, Granola. [builtinnyc.com](https://www.builtinnyc.com/articles/rise-startup-studios-new-york-city)
- **Success rate**: **Unclear/not disclosed** as a percentage.
- **Notable failures**: **Unclear/not disclosed** — no specific internally-built shutdowns detailed in sources.
- **Profit structure**: Equity-stake model — founding/large stakes in internally-built companies, standard VC minority stakes in fund investments. [builtinnyc.com](https://www.builtinnyc.com/articles/rise-startup-studios-new-york-city)
- **Moat**: 15+ years NYC tech relationships; sustained AI thesis nearly a decade pre-2023 boom (seeded Hugging Face, Stability AI pre-seed/seed); Camp cohort infrastructure. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2024/10/30/betaworks-focuses-on-ai-applications-in-its-latest-camp/)
- **Hiring**: Open application into "Camp" 12-week in-residence cohorts (betaworks.com/camp/application), not a classic 1:1 EIR model. [betaworks.com](https://www.betaworks.com/camp/application)
- **Org structure**: Borthwick as CEO/founder; studio + Camp accelerator + multiple discrete funds (I/II/III, Betaworks Ventures). [betaworks.com](https://www.betaworks.com/team)
- **Investment criteria**: AI Camp cohorts target tools that "augment the way humans behave, create, play, work and think"; 2024 cohorts selected "agentic" B2B, personalized software, hardware-enabled AI. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2023/04/13/betaworks-new-camp-aims-to-fund-transformative-early-stage-ai-startups/) [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2024/04/16/betaworks-bets-on-ai-agents-in-latest-camp-cohort/)
- **AI usage (2023–2026)**: Dedicated AI "Camp" cohorts every year since 2023: (1) 2023 AI Camp — $500K to ~10 companies, June–Sept 2023, compute from Hugging Face/Stability AI; (2) April 2024 — 9 AI-agent startups; (3) October 2024 — 10 "AI interface" startups; (4) Fund III ($66M, closed July 2025) explicitly targets early-stage AI. Frames approach as "thesis-based investing" with nearly a decade of pre-boom AI thesis. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2023/04/13/betaworks-new-camp-aims-to-fund-transformative-early-stage-ai-startups/) [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2025/07/22/betaworks-third-fund-closes-at-66m-to-invest-in-early-stage-ai-startups/)

### Atomic (Jack Abraham, San Francisco)

- **Overview**: SF venture studio. Unlike a typical VC, only funds companies it builds/spins up itself via an internal "Zero to One" team — no outside-startup investing. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2021/03/02/atomic-which-only-funds-the-startups-it-launches-just-closed-its-newest-fund-with-260-million/)
- **History**: Founded 2012 by Jack Abraham. Initial $20M fund → $260M fund (announced March 2021) → $320M fourth fund (added a former Postmates CFO as GP). [en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jack_Abraham) [finance.yahoo.com](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/exclusive-venture-studio-atomic-raises-113000211.html)
- **Founders**: Jack Abraham — founded Milo.com (price comparison, acquired by eBay 2010 for ~$75M). Andrew Dudum co-founded specific portfolio companies (e.g., Hims) alongside Abraham. [en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jack_Abraham) [en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hims_%26_Hers_Health)
- **Funding**: LPs reportedly include Peter Thiel and Marc Andreessen. Progression: $20M (2012) → $260M (March 2021) → $320M (fourth fund). [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2021/03/02/atomic-which-only-funds-the-startups-it-launches-just-closed-its-newest-fund-with-260-million/) [finance.yahoo.com](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/exclusive-venture-studio-atomic-raises-113000211.html)
- **Companies launched**: 14 startups launched in the 12 months prior to Nov 2021. Named: Hims & Hers Health (NYSE: HIMS), Homebound, Bungalow, Found, OpenStore, Replicant, Exowatt. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2021/11/17/talking-atomic/)
- **Exits**: **Hims & Hers Health** — founded Nov 2017 by Andrew Dudum, Jack Abraham, Hilary Coles inside Atomic; SPAC merger w/ Oaktree Acquisition Corp, Jan 20 2021, $1.6B valuation (NYSE: HIMS); Atomic retained a "material minority stake," reported ~20-30%. [en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hims_%26_Hers_Health) [forbes.com](https://www.forbes.com/sites/katiejennings/2021/01/20/digital-health-startup-hims--hers-goes-public-in-16-billion-spac-deal/)
- **Success rate**: **Unclear/not disclosed** as a formal percentage.
- **Notable failures**: **Unclear/not disclosed** for Atomic-founded companies specifically.
- **Profit structure**: Co-founds and typically retains meaningful equity (reported 20-30%+ in Hims case) rather than charging fees. [en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hims_%26_Hers_Health)
- **Moat**: "Zero to One" internal team identifies/rigorously validates well-defined market problems before recruiting/co-founding — reducing idea risk before capital/founder time committed. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2021/11/17/talking-atomic/)
- **Hiring**: "Founder-in-Residence" and "Engineer-in-Residence" programs; rolling applications; screening call → interviews with core collaborators → virtual working session. Works with ~10–15 new founders/year. [atomic.vc](https://www.atomic.vc/co-found) [johngannonblog.com](https://johngannonblog.com/vc-fellowship-atomic-in-san-francisco-ca/)
- **Org structure**: Multiple GPs (incl. a former Postmates CFO for Fund IV) alongside Abraham; distinct FIR/EIR tracks; dedicated "Zero to One" team. [finance.yahoo.com](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/exclusive-venture-studio-atomic-raises-113000211.html)
- **Investment criteria**: "Well-defined problems" in large markets, rigorously tested/validated via Zero-to-One before co-founding. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2021/11/17/talking-atomic/)
- **AI usage (2023–2026)**: Internal AI-tooling disclosures **not found — unclear/not disclosed**. Recent portfolio (Exowatt, Replicant — AI customer service) reflects an AI-forward thesis. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2021/11/17/talking-atomic/)

### Rocket Internet (Samwer brothers, Berlin)

- **Overview**: Berlin global "company builder"/holdco, historically known for building fast-scaling replicas ("copycats") of proven internet models in underserved markets. [en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rocket_Internet)
- **History**: Founded 2007 by brothers Marc, Oliver, Alexander Samwer. Prior exits funding Rocket: Alando (German eBay clone) sold to eBay 1999 for $43M; Jamba! sold to VeriSign 2004 for $273M. Founded Zalando 2008 (explicitly emulating Zappos.com). IPO'd Frankfurt Stock Exchange Oct 2 2014 at €42.50/share (>€6.7B valuation). Delisted Sept 1 2020 after prolonged sub-NAV trading. April 2024: Global Founders Capital (GFC) formally became Rocket's dedicated venture arm, deploying balance-sheet capital instead of a third outside fund. [en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rocket_Internet) [forbes.com](https://www.forbes.com/sites/ryanmac/2014/07/31/samwer-brothers-billionaires-rocket-internet-ipo/) [fortune.com](https://fortune.com/2020/09/01/rocket-internet-startup-incubator-germany-europe-tech-industry/) [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2024/04/26/global-founders-capital-becomes-rocket-internets-venture-arm/)
- **Founders**: Marc, Oliver, Alexander Samwer; Oliver remains CEO. [forbes.com](https://www.forbes.com/profile/oliver-samwer/)
- **Funding**: Pre-IPO raised $445M from Philippine Long Distance Telephone Co. and $582M from United Internet. "Rocket Internet Capital Partners" closed a $1B fund Jan 2017 (largest European tech VC fund at the time). Post-restructuring (April 2024), GFC had ~€300M on Rocket's balance sheet (having previously raised two separate $1B GFC funds). As of May 2025, Rocket reportedly held >€1.1B liquidity cushion — **sourced from a lower-confidence SEO aggregator (matrixbcg.com); treat as indicative, not fully verified**. [en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rocket_Internet) [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2024/04/26/global-founders-capital-becomes-rocket-internets-venture-arm/)
- **Companies launched**: As of May 2025, portfolio comprised 164 companies incl. 7 identified unicorns — **sourced from a lower-confidence aggregator, not independently corroborated via primary disclosure**.
- **Exits**: **Zalando** — flagship, now independent Frankfurt-listed company. **Jumia** — African e-commerce, IPO'd NYSE 2019; Rocket sold remaining 11% stake April 2020 amid mounting losses. Retained/retains stakes in **Home24** and **Global Fashion Group**. GFC's first fund projected to return 3-4x; **Pennylane** (French fintech, entered pre-seed, now >€1B) cited as standout. [technext.ng](https://technext.ng/2020/04/03/jumia-loses-major-investor-hours-after-lagos-govt-shut-down-its-warehouse-for-violating-lockdown-directive/) [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2024/04/26/global-founders-capital-becomes-rocket-internets-venture-arm/)
- **Success rate**: **Unclear/not disclosed** as explicit percentage; GFC's 3-4x projected fund-level return multiple is the closest proxy.
- **Notable failures**: Jumia's post-IPO trajectory repeatedly described as troubled (mounting losses, downsizing, African-market exits) though still listed. Rocket criticized for years for high-burn "copycat" model; stated shift to path-to-profitability within 18–24 months for new investments as of 2025 (**lower-confidence source**). [tekedia.com](https://www.tekedia.com/how-germanys-rocket-internet-built-and-exited-jumia-with-possible-loss/)
- **Profit structure**: Holdco/company-builder equity model — significant founding equity stakes via seed capital/shared services/recruiting; monetizes via IPOs, trade sales, dividends from mature holdings (e.g., Zalando).
- **Moat**: Deep operational playbook for rapid international replication/scaling of proven models; large shared cross-portfolio services org; Samwer brand/network incl. "Rocket alumni." [en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rocket_Internet)
- **Hiring**: Historically aggressive recruiting/management culture, parachuting experienced operators into new ventures; EIR-style program details **unclear/not disclosed**.
- **Org structure**: Rocket Internet SE (public/formerly public holdco) + Global Founders Capital as venture-investing arm (5 partners as of April 2024: Fabricio Pettena, Don Stalter, Cedric Asselman, [name garbled in source], Oliver Samwer). [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2024/04/26/global-founders-capital-becomes-rocket-internets-venture-arm/)
- **Investment criteria (post-2024)**: Geographic shift toward deep-tier emerging markets (MENA, Southeast Asia); sector focus AI-driven SaaS, marketplace verticals w/ visible unit economics, fintech, logistics tech; late-2024 ~€350M fund targeting Saudi Vision 2030 — **sourced from a lower-confidence secondary aggregator, not independently cross-verified**.
- **AI usage (2023–2026)**: Investment focus shifted to include AI-driven SaaS; floated SPAC-style vehicles ("Rocket Internet Growth Opportunities Corp") targeting enterprise software/AI deals outside the US — **sourced from a low-confidence aggregator (toolify.ai), could not corroborate; recommend independent verification**. No detail found on Rocket's *internal* operational AI use — **unclear/not disclosed**.

[tech.eu](https://tech.eu/2022/11/30/efounders-rebrands-to-hexa/) [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2023/10/30/hexa-the-startup-studio-behind-front-spendesk-and-aircall-unveils-its-next-batch-of-startups/)

Portfolio-level figure: €700M raised across all Hexa/eFounders-built companies to date (per Hexa's own track-record account). [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2023/11/27/paris-based-startup-studio-hexa-raises-some-funding-to-launch-even-more-startups/)

- **Overview**: Canadian, publicly-listed (TSX: CSU) serial acquirer of vertical market software (VMS) businesses — software serving a specific narrow industry niche (e.g., library systems, funeral-home software, court records). ### Berkshire Hathaway (Warren Buffett / Charlie Munger capital allocation model)

- **Overview**: Holding company combining (a) wholly-owned operating subsidiaries across insurance, rail, energy, manufacturing, retail, with (b) a large public-equities portfolio, funded substantially by **insurance float** — premiums collected before claims are paid, invested for Berkshire's own benefit at (historically) negative cost given underwriting-profitable insurance ops.

### Japan Market Context (not a company profile — background for market-entry analysis)

**Startup funding environment 2024–2026**
- Total Japan startup fundraising (equity, excl. debt): **2024: ¥779.3bn; 2025: ¥761.3bn** (roughly flat YoY); number of fundraising companies fell ~6% to ~2,700 in 2025; median deal size fell from ¥77.6M (2024) to ¥62.4M (2025) — a "flight to quality"/selectivity trend rather than market collapse. [initial.inc](https://initial.inc/articles/japan-startup-finance-2025)
- H1 2025 fundraising: **¥339.9bn**, up ~4% YoY.
- By funder type (2025): pure VC investment declined 8% to ¥297.3bn; **corporate (CVC) investment rose to ¥148bn** (+¥32bn); financial-institution investment rose to ¥36.7bn (from ¥31bn) — growing corporate/CVC appetite relative to traditional VC, directly relevant to a studio model seeking corporate JV/LP partners. [chambers.com](https://practiceguides.chambers.com/practice-guides/venture-capital-2025/japan/trends-and-developments)
- Series A funding fell from ¥168.4bn (2024) to ¥139.1bn (2025), deal count down (519→501); Series B rose in total yen (¥172.6bn→¥192.4bn) but deal count fell (351→312) — capital concentrating into fewer, larger later-stage-of-early deals.
- **J-Startup program** (METI): selects high-potential startups via VC/corporate-nominator panel for concentrated public+private support; latest cohort announced March 2025.
- **Government "Startup Development Five-Year Plan" targets for FY2027**: grow to 100,000 startups, nurture 100 unicorns, attract ¥10 trillion in cumulative startup investment.
- JIC (Japan Investment Corporation), a government-linked fund-of-funds, remains an active LP/policy vehicle in this ecosystem.

**Labor market factors relevant to a studio model**
- **IT/digital talent shortage**: METI estimates a shortfall of up to **790,000 IT workers by 2030**, with an existing shortfall of roughly **430,000 as of 2025**.
- **Side-jobs/secondary employment (副業・兼業)**: A national policy push to legitimize corporate employees taking side projects/second jobs; a 2018-era matching program between big-company talent and regional SMEs produced 109 matches, 66 via side-job/secondary-employment arrangements — a real but still small-scale channel.
- **Foreign tech talent/visas**: Foreign nationals under the "Engineer/Specialist in Humanities/International Services" visa status reached **418,706 people at end of 2024**, up 56,360 YoY. Note: stricter visa-screening procedures flagged from April 2026 for this category, which could tighten future foreign-hiring flexibility.
- **Lifetime-employment culture shift**: Continued gradual erosion of traditional lifetime employment norms among younger/urban professionals (from search synthesis, not independently re-verified with a dedicated primary source), with DeNA's "Springboard"/"official career path to entrepreneurship" framing being a direct corporate response/case study.

**Regulatory considerations for AI companies in Japan**
- **AI事業者ガイドライン (AI Business Operator Guidelines)**: Jointly issued by METI and the Ministry of Internal Affairs and Communications. **v1.0 released April 19, 2024** as non-binding "soft law"; **v1.1 released March 28, 2025**; **v1.2 released March 31, 2026** — an actively iterating "living document," not a fixed statute. Targets three actor classes: AI developers, AI providers, AI users. Developed via multi-stakeholder process (academia, civil society, industry).
- **Practical implication for a studio**: Japan currently favors soft-law/guideline-based AI governance (vs. the EU's hard-law AI Act model) — lower near-term compliance burden for an AI studio launching multiple ventures quickly, though this could tighten as the guideline matures.

**Unique advantages Japan offers an AI startup studio**
- Corporate JV/CVC appetite rising, not falling (2025 corporate investment +¥32bn YoY even as pure VC contracted -8%).
- Government subsidy/compute support specifically for generative AI (GENIAC — reported ¥8.4bn allocation for one tranche via Google Cloud, dataset support, structured matching).
- Robotics/hardware ecosystem: major corporates (Toyota, KDDI, NEC, Fujitsu, Mitsubishi Electric, GMO Internet Group) formal members of the **AI Robot Association (AIRoA)**, launched March 2025, for shared robotics data infrastructure — a hard-tech/physical-AI ecosystem with deep corporate co-investment appetite.
- Talent-cost/demographic factors: structural IT-talent shortage is double-edged — raises domestic engineering costs but increases corporate willingness to fund/partner with external studios/CVCs and openness to foreign engineering talent under eased-then-tightening visa regimes.
- Existing playbook density: Japan already has multiple functioning studio/company-builder precedents (Delight Ventures/DeNA, Relic/ZERO1000, quantum/Hakuhodo, XTech) that a new AI-focused entrant can benchmark, partner with, or differentiate against.
