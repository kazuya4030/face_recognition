## 1. Founders Factory (London)

- **Overview**: Hybrid corporate venture studio + accelerator. Runs two parallel tracks: a Venture Studio that co-founds new companies from scratch with entrepreneurs-in-residence, and thematic Accelerator programs run jointly with named corporate partners. [foundersfactory.com/studio](https://foundersfactory.com/studio/), [foundersfactory.com](https://foundersfactory.com/)
- **History**: Founded by Henry Lane Fox and Brent Hoberman (also co-founder of lastminute.com and Founders Forum). Named the UK's top-ranked startup hub and a top-5 European venture studio by the Financial Times in 2025. Expanded to Founders Factory Africa (deploying $114M, per a 2023 TechCrunch report on new fund learnings). [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2023/08/16/founders-factory-africa-to-deploy-114m-using-learnings-from-past-programs/)
- **Founders/Leaders**: Henry Lane Fox, Brent Hoberman.
- **Funding raised**: ~€300M raised from corporate partners including Mediobanca, Deutsche Telekom, Fastweb, Johnson & Johnson, easyJet; separately reported $26.9M raised across two rounds for global operations (Crunchbase-sourced figure, treat as approximate). [startupintros.com](https://startupintros.com/orgs/founders-factory)
- **Companies launched**: Portfolio of 300+ companies; builds ~15 new enterprises and accelerates ~40 startups per year via studio + accelerator tracks combined. [ellenox.com](https://www.ellenox.com/post/top-10-venture-studios), [startupintros.com](https://startupintros.com/orgs/founders-factory)
- **Exits/notable outcomes**: $900M+ in follow-on funding raised by portfolio companies since inception; specific acquisition-exit count unclear from available sources.
- **Success rate**: Not disclosed in sourced materials — unclear.
- **Notable failures**: Not disclosed in available sources — unclear.
- **Revenue/profit structure**: Corporate partners pay into dedicated program funds; Founders Factory takes 5–7% equity in startups in exchange for capital plus ~6 months of intensive operational/strategic support. [startupintros.com](https://startupintros.com/orgs/founders-factory)
- **Competitive advantage/moat**: Deep corporate distribution relationships (Aviva, L'Oréal, Guardian Media Group, easyJet, Reckitt, M&S, HSBC, Rio Tinto, Northwestern Medicine) that embed portfolio companies into real supply chains/customer bases from idea stage; 60-person operator team across London, Chicago, Perth, Singapore. [foundersfactory.com/london](https://foundersfactory.com/london/)
- **Hiring approach**: Recruits "Venture Designers" and operator-entrepreneurs to co-found with corporate partners; roles posted publicly (e.g., Venture Designer, London). [jobsatventurestudios.com](https://jobsatventurestudios.com/discover/companies/founders-factory/jobs/75589032-venture-designer-london)
- **Org structure**: Dual-track org — Studio (company creation) and Accelerator (external startup investment), overlaid with sector/partner-specific programs (fintech, climate, health, deep tech in London).
- **Investment criteria**: Early/pre-seed to seed stage; thematic fit with corporate partner sector (fintech, climate, health, deep tech in London).
- **AI usage in process (critical focus)**: Founders Factory has published detailed first-person accounts of deploying agentic AI systems across its ventures. Tech Lead Jack Howell and Product Director Jacob George describe 14 months of hands-on agentic-system deployment in regulated sectors (insurance, finance), concluding that generic agent frameworks fail and each function/industry needs specialized, tool-scoped agents with human-in-the-loop oversight. Reported results in fintech/insurance deployments: onboarding cut from 12 weeks to 1 week, due diligence cost cut from £50k to £1k, and deal-flow capacity increased from 2 to 40 programs/year (a 20x claimed improvement). They also publish a "Venture Building with AI Agents" thought-leadership series discussing using AI throughout the studio pipeline — AI-generated code prototyping, automated market research, and agent-assisted growth hacking. [foundersfactory.substack.com](https://foundersfactory.substack.com/p/venture-building-with-ai-agents-new), [foundersfactory.com/articles/venture-building-with-ai-agent](https://foundersfactory.com/articles/venture-building-with-ai-agent/)

Began gaming-focused (56% of the 25-company debut 2023 cohort were game studios); pivoted hard toward AI as the wave hit — by cohort SR006 (wrapped spring 2026), 87% of the 60 companies carried an AI tag. Now runs two cohorts per year. [genaiassembling.substack.com](https://genaiassembling.substack.com/p/speedruns-pivot-is-the-playbook-what), [fortune.com](https://fortune.com/2025/04/02/why-a16zs-speedrun-accelerator-is-playing-a-new-game/)

- **Funding raised**: Third Speedrun cohort backed by a dedicated $75M accelerator fund (a16z Games). Program invests up to $1M per company plus up to $5M in credits (including AI compute). [games.gg](https://games.gg/news/a16z-opens-third-speedrun-75-million-accelerator/), [gamedeveloper.com](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/a16z-games-commits-75m-to-accelerator-program-for-early-game-startups)

- **Exits/notable outcomes**: Not comprehensively disclosed in sourced materials — unclear; program is still young (2023–2026).
- **Success rate**: Not disclosed — unclear.
- **Notable failures**: Not disclosed — unclear.
- **Revenue/profit structure**: Standard accelerator equity-for-capital-and-credits model; a16z takes equity stake per participating company.
- **Competitive advantage/moat**: a16z's brand, LP network, and access to 1,000+ investors at Demo Day in San Francisco; deep compute-credit relationships (AI compute partners) that are highly relevant to the current AI-native cohort.
- **Hiring approach**: N/A (accelerator, not employer of company teams) — selects founder teams via competitive application.
- **Org structure**: Embedded within a16z Games as a sub-fund/program; 12-week intensive cohort format.
- **Investment criteria**: Originally game studios; now "AI broadly," per genaiassembling.substack.com's analysis of the pivot — assessed on team + prototype/demo strength within a 12-week timeline.

No specific disclosure found of a16z using AI agents internally to run Speedrun operations — unclear/not found in available sources.

## 3. Neo (Ali Partovi)

- **Overview**: Talent-first venture firm and fellowship ("Neo Scholars") that identifies elite technical talent (often pre-company, sometimes still in college) and backs them with capital, mentorship, and a dense peer/mentor network rather than starting from a specific startup thesis. [neo.substack.com](https://neo.substack.com/p/meet-the-2025-neo-scholars)
- **History**: Founded 2017 by Ali Partovi (Code.org co-founder, early Facebook/Dropbox/Airbnb investor) following an early scouting idea dating to 2016. Eight-plus years running as of 2025 profiles. [forbes.com](https://www.forbes.com/sites/bizcarson/2019/01/18/an-early-facebook-investor-is-creating-a-scouting-network-for-brilliant-engineers/), [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2025/04/27/from-coding-tests-to-billion-dollar-startups-ali-partovis-eight-year-experiment-is-paying-off/)
- **Founders/Leaders**: Ali Partovi (founder); partners Suzanne Xie and Emily Cohen run the Neo Scholars program. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2025/04/27/from-coding-tests-to-billion-dollar-startups-ali-partovis-eight-year-experiment-is-paying-off/)
- **Funding raised**: Fund IV closed at $320M (largest to date, closed ~April 2025); Neo has raised $600M+ cumulative across all funds, with reported AUM of $1B+. [tbpndigest.com](https://www.tbpndigest.com/story/2025-04-16/ali-partovi-raises-320m-fund-iv-for-neo-silicon-valleys-most-selective-college-talent-incubator), [eaglerockcfo.com](https://www.eaglerockcfo.com/blog/venture-capital-firms/neo-ventures-review)
- **Companies launched/backed**: Neo Scholars accepts ~20–30 students/year (from thousands of applicants) via a $20,000 no-equity gap-semester grant; broader portfolio spans 160+ companies. [neo.substack.com](https://neo.substack.com/p/neo-scholar-applications-are-open-ca9)
- **Exits/notable outcomes**: Aggregate portfolio value reported at $110B+ with reported IRR of 51% since inception (Eagle Rock CFO analysis). Marquee wins: Cursor/Anysphere (Neo's ~$4M combined stake in Cursor + Kalshi reportedly worth ~$1.4B as of Q1 2026); Cursor's parent Anysphere is being acquired by SpaceX for $60B in an all-stock deal announced June 16, 2026, days after SpaceX's IPO (confirmed by CNBC, Bloomberg, TechCrunch, Axios, CBS News). Also backed Cognition (valued ~$4B), Kalshi, Bluesky (early outside investor, ~$700M valuation round), Pika, and Chai Discovery. [x.com/apartovi](https://x.com/apartovi/status/1912541308536910094), [cnbc.com](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/16/spacex-spcx-cursor-acquisition-ipo.html), [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/16/spacex-to-acquire-cursor-for-60b-in-stock-days-after-blockbuster-ipo/)
- **Success rate**: Every one of OpenAI's new-grad hires in a recent year was reportedly a former Neo Scholar (TechCrunch), used as an informal talent-quality signal rather than a startup success rate. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2025/04/27/from-coding-tests-to-billion-dollar-startups-ali-partovis-eight-year-experiment-is-paying-off/)
- **Notable failures**: Not disclosed in sourced materials — unclear.
- **Revenue/profit structure**: Traditional fund economics (management fee + carry) on Scholars/seed investments; separately, the no-equity $20K Scholars grants are philanthropic/brand-building rather than direct-return vehicles.
- **Competitive advantage/moat**: Extremely selective talent-scouting network (170+ industry veteran mentors), multi-year relationship-building with individuals pre-founding, and a "low-dilution" accelerator-style term sheet reported by TechCrunch as disrupting the standard accelerator model. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/19/ali-partovis-neo-looks-to-upend-the-accelerator-model-with-low-dilution-terms/)
- **Hiring approach**: N/A in the studio-employee sense; Neo "hires" scholars into a fellowship/mentorship cohort rather than staffing companies directly.
- **Org structure**: Fund (Neo) + fellowship program (Neo Scholars) run in parallel; mentor network of 170+ industry veterans supplements the small core partner team.
- **Investment criteria**: Talent-first, pre-idea; looks for exceptional individuals (often still in college) rather than a specific sector thesis.
- **AI usage in process**: Not disclosed as a formal internal tool in sourced materials — unclear. Neo's relevance to AI is overwhelmingly as an *investor in* AI-native companies (Cursor, Cognition, Pika) rather than as a user of AI/agents in its own scouting or company-building operations.

## 5. AI-coding-agent-native builders: Cursor/Anysphere (tool company) + Alloy Partners' "agentic venture studio" (studio model)

### 5a. Cursor / Anysphere (context: AI coding tool company, not a studio)

- **Overview**: Anysphere, Inc. ("Cursor") builds an AI-native code editor / coding agent used heavily by both individual developers and (relevantly) by venture studios as an internal tool. [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cursor_(company)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cursor_(company))
- **History**: Founded early 2022 by four MIT students — Michael Truell (CEO), Sualeh Asif, Aman Sanger, Arvid Lunnemark; Cursor editor launched March 2023.
- **Funding**: Seed ($8M, OpenAI Startup Fund, 2023) → Series A ($60M @ $400M, Aug 2024, a16z) → Series B ($105M @ $2.5B, Dec 2024, Thrive/a16z) → Series C ($900M @ $9.9B, June 2025) → Series D ($2.3B @ $29.3B, Nov 13 2025, Accel/Coatue, with Google and Nvidia participating). [cnbc.com](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/13/cursor-ai-startup-funding-round-valuation.html)
- **2026 acquisition**: SpaceX agreed to acquire Anysphere for **$60B in an all-stock deal**, announced June 16, 2026, days after SpaceX's own record-setting Nasdaq IPO; deal intended to bolster SpaceX/xAI's AI ambitions; expected to close Q3 2026. Confirmed across CNBC, Bloomberg, TechCrunch, Axios, CBS News. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/16/spacex-to-acquire-cursor-for-60b-in-stock-days-after-blockbuster-ipo/), [bloomberg.com](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-16/spacex-cements-60-billion-deal-to-take-over-ai-startup-cursor)
- **Revenue growth**: ARR grew from ~$100M (Jan 2025) → $500M (June 2025) → $1B (Nov 2025) → $2B (Feb 2026) → ~$4B (June 2026) — among the fastest-scaling B2B software companies ever. [tech-insider.org](https://tech-insider.org/cursor-60-billion-valuation-anysphere-ai-coding-2026/)
- **Relevance to studios**: Not itself a venture studio; included per the brief as the leading AI coding-agent tool that studios (Founders Factory, Alloy Partners, Forum Ventures) use as infrastructure inside their own build pipelines.

### 5b. Alloy Partners — "agentic venture studio" (most legitimate example of a studio built explicitly around AI agents)

- **Overview**: Indianapolis-based venture builder that in 2025 launched what it and trade press describe as one of the first "agentic venture studios" — using an orchestrated architecture of 1,000+ specialized AI agents in place of traditional early-stage studio headcount to build multiple companies simultaneously. [alloypartners.com/articles/agentic-venture-studio-building-nine-companies-with-1000-ai-agents](https://www.alloypartners.com/articles/agentic-venture-studio-building-nine-companies-with-1000-ai-agents)
- **History**: Alloy Partners is the March 2025 rebrand of **High Alpha Innovation** (itself spun out in 2020 from High Alpha, Indianapolis — see entry 6 below), reflecting a strategic pivot to explicit corporate-startup co-creation "studios-as-a-service." [techpoint.org](https://techpoint.org/digital-innovation/high-alpha-innovation-rebrands-as-alloy-partners-strengthening-commitment-to-corporate-startup-collaboration/), [prnewswire.com](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/high-alpha-innovation-rebrands-as-alloy-partners-strengthening-commitment-to-corporate-startup-collaboration-302402133.html)
- **Founders/Leaders**: Elliott Parker, CEO — background in strategy consulting at Innosight (Clayton Christensen's firm) before joining High Alpha to lead business design/corporate innovation. [prnewswire.com](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/high-alpha-innovation-rebrands-as-alloy-partners-strengthening-commitment-to-corporate-startup-collaboration-302402133.html)
- **Funding raised**: Inherited High Alpha's track record of $350M+ raised across four funds (as the originating team); Alloy Partners itself operates a multi-partner studio model funded per-partnership (e.g., with Elanco Animal Health and the State of Indiana as founding collaborators of "OneHealth Studio," and a separate studio launched with Huntington Bank for banking innovation). [prnewswire.com/OneHealth](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/alloy-partners-launches-onehealth-studio-to-create-startups-at-the-convergence-of-animal-plant-and-human-health-302613830.html), [alloypartners.com/Huntington](https://www.alloypartners.com/articles/alloy-partners-huntington-bank-launch-banking-venture-studio)
- **Companies launched**: Reported nine concepts built simultaneously spanning biotech, AgTech, and health technology under the agentic studio model.
- **Exits/notable outcomes**: Not yet disclosed — program is very new (2025) — unclear.
- **Success rate**: Not disclosed — unclear. Notably, the studio openly discloses an AI-agent *output-quality* rate: roughly 90% solid output vs. 10% requiring human correction, underscoring that human-in-the-loop review remains structurally required.
- **Notable failures**: Not disclosed — unclear.
- **Revenue/profit structure**: Multi-partner studio model — corporate/institutional partners (e.g., Elanco, State of Indiana, Huntington Bank) co-fund studio operations in exchange for stakes/strategic access to companies created.
- **Competitive advantage/moat**: Proprietary AI-agent "swarm" architecture — 1,133 AI agents organized across seven operational pillars (regulatory/clinical, market/competitive intelligence, financial/fundraising, marketing/brand, product/engineering, operations/legal, knowledge/tools), coordinated by a "principal intelligence orchestrator." Each new portfolio company reportedly gets a full virtual team from day one (regulatory specialists, general counsel, domain experts, data analysts) before any human hires are made.
- **Hiring approach**: Human team functions primarily as supervisors/reviewers of agent output rather than first-line executors; suggests a leaner human headcount per company than the traditional venture-studio model.
- **Org structure**: Studio-of-studios model — multiple named sub-studios (OneHealth Studio with Elanco; a banking studio with Huntington Bank) each with dedicated corporate/institutional co-founders, layered on the shared agentic infrastructure.
- **Investment criteria**: Thesis-driven by partner vertical (animal/plant/human health convergence with Elanco; banking/fintech with Huntington).
- **AI usage in process (critical focus)**: This is Alloy Partners' core differentiator — described in its own long-form piece as "the first agentic venture studio," explicitly built to replace traditional early-stage studio headcount with an agent architecture rather than merely using AI as a productivity add-on. [alloypartners.com](https://www.alloypartners.com/articles/agentic-venture-studio-building-nine-companies-with-1000-ai-agents)

## 6. High Alpha (Indianapolis)

- **Overview**: Venture studio + venture capital firm pioneering the modern "studio" model for B2B SaaS company creation. Two arms: **High Alpha Studio** (conceives/launches/scales companies with design, finance, talent, marketing, GTM services) and **High Alpha Capital** (invests in Studio companies and other emerging B2B SaaS startups). [highalpha.com](https://www.highalpha.com/), [highalpha.com/studio](https://www.highalpha.com/studio)
- **History**: Founded 2015 by Scott Dorsey (former ExactTarget CEO), Eric Tobias, Mike Fitzgerald, and Kristian Andersen. Opened its third dedicated venture studio fund in October 2021, at which point it had co-founded 30+ companies. [businesswire.com](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20211006005132/en/High-Alpha-Launches-Its-Third-Venture-Studio-Building-on-Momentum-After-Co-Founding-Over-30-New-Companies), [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2021/10/06/high-alpha-opens-third-venture-studio-co-founder-calls-venture-market-hot-and-crazy/)
- **Founders/Leaders**: Scott Dorsey, Eric Tobias, Mike Fitzgerald, Kristian Andersen.
- **Funding raised**: High Alpha Capital runs a $110M fund investing pre-seed through Series B (typical check size $1–5M) in the US/Canada. [highalpha.com](https://www.highalpha.com/)
- **Companies launched**: 45+ companies co-founded through the Studio as of most recent figures (30+ as of the Oct 2021 third-studio launch, growing since); notable companies include Lessonly, Zylo, Visible, Tenon, MetaCX, Mandolin, Casted, Bolster. At any given time, the Studio is reportedly building around five companies in parallel. [highalpha.com/companies](https://www.highalpha.com/companies)
- **Exits/notable outcomes**: Seismic acquired Lessonly (High Alpha's training/coaching/enablement company) — a confirmed exit. Reported seven total exits as of October 2021 across the portfolio. [ibj.com](https://www.ibj.com/articles/68078-more-than-money)
- **Success rate**: Not formally disclosed as a percentage — unclear.
- **Notable failures**: Not disclosed in sourced materials — unclear.
- **Revenue/profit structure**: Studio takes founder-level equity in companies it co-creates; Capital arm invests separately for additional equity/returns; blended company-building services + fund economics model that other studios (e.g., Alloy Partners, above) have since imitated and rebranded.
- **Competitive advantage/moat**: First-mover/model-pioneer status in the venture studio category ("pioneered the venture studio model" per multiple sources); deep Indianapolis B2B SaaS ecosystem relationships; reusable playbook, infrastructure, and shared services (design, finance, talent, marketing, GTM) across parallel company builds.
- **Hiring approach**: Not fully detailed in sourced materials, but the model implies embedding a shared studio team across multiple simultaneous company builds, then recruiting dedicated founder/CEO talent per company at inception — unclear on exact hiring mechanics beyond this.
- **Org structure**: Two-arm structure (Studio + Capital) with shared brand/infrastructure but separate investment vehicles.
- **Investment criteria**: B2B SaaS-focused but sector-agnostic within that band; has funded ecommerce, supply chain/logistics, future-of-work, and enterprise software companies. [highalpha.com/resources/techstars-alternative](https://www.highalpha.com/resources/techstars-alternative)
- **AI usage in process**: No specific, sourced disclosure of formal AI/LLM tooling inside High Alpha's own Studio pipeline was found via direct search of highalpha.com (site blocked WebFetch with 403) or via general web search — **unclear/not found**. Notably, High Alpha's own spun-out sister entity, Alloy Partners (High Alpha Innovation's 2025 rebrand — see entry 5b), is the org in this family that has gone public with a detailed AI-agent architecture; High Alpha's core Studio itself does not appear to have published comparable AI-process disclosures as of this research.
