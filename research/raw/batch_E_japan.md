# Batch E: AI Startup Studios / Venture Studios / Company Builders — Japan

Research date: 2026-07-13. Compiled for a Japan market-entry strategy report on AI startup studios. Sources are cited inline; "unclear/not disclosed" is used where figures could not be verified from public sources.

## 1. DeNA — Internal New-Business Incubation ("Venture Builder" via Delight Ventures)

- **Overview / model type**: DeNA does not use the label "DeNA X." Its actual mechanism is a hybrid of (a) internal new-business proposal systems inside DeNA itself, and (b) an externalized "Venture Builder" business run through **Delight Ventures**, an independent VC founded by DeNA chairman **Tomoko Namba (南場智子)**. DeNA calls itself a "永久ベンチャー" (perpetual venture) and pursues new business both via internal M&A/incubation and via Delight Ventures' builder model. [DeNA CSR/Venture](https://csr.dena.com/jp/venture/), [DeNA news release](https://dena.com/jp/news/4498/)
- **History**: Delight Ventures established with a ¥10 billion-class ("100億円規模") "Delight Ventures 1号投資事業有限責任組合" fund and launched the "ベンチャー・ビルダー事業" (Venture Builder business) explicitly to spin out businesses and produce entrepreneurs from inside/outside DeNA — announced 2019. [DeNA news](https://dena.com/jp/news/4498/), [Nikkei 2019](https://www.nikkei.com/article/DGXLRSP515234_V20C19A7000000/). In Oct 2021 Delight Ventures opened its Venture Builder externally ("1st V-Challe") to non-DeNA entrepreneurs. [TheBridge 2021](https://thebridge.jp/2021/10/delight-ventures-announces-1st-v-challenge). In Nov 2025, Delight Ventures formed a new **¥5 billion ("50億円") "Delight Ventures Builder 3 (DVB3)" fund**, with DeNA as sole LP, focused on AI-driven "Vertical Shift," "Momentum Shift," and "US cross-border" entrepreneurship, plus two new zero-to-one incubation programs "V-ShIP" and "M-ShIP." [PR Times](https://prtimes.jp/main/html/rd/p/000000027.000052552.html), [TheBridge 2025](https://thebridge.jp/2025/11/delight-ventures-launches-5b-yen-fund-ai-startups-dena-backing), [Delight Ventures Insights](https://www.delight-ventures.com/insights/venture-builder-introduce2)
- **Founders/leaders**: Tomoko Namba (DeNA founder/chairman, also founder of Delight Ventures); DeNA also separately acquired Knot, Inc. (AI recruiting platform "Noxx"), with founder Kiyotaka Kobayashi (小林清剛) joining DeNA to lead new business creation and startup investment. [DeNA news (AI Link)](https://dena.com/intl/news/4753/)
- **Funding raised**: Delight Ventures 1号 fund ≈¥10bn (2019); DVB3 fund ¥5bn (2025, DeNA as sole LP). Cumulative Builder-fund investments: 30+ companies domestically and internationally as of 2025. [note.com studio series](https://note.com/susu_media/n/ne7d87ad63071)
- **Companies/businesses launched**: 30+ companies via the Builder fund cumulative (domestic + overseas) as of 2025 reporting. Historical internal data point: one DeNA new-business team reported "24 businesses launched, 19 closed" over 3 years using lean-startup methodology (illustrative of DeNA's internal churn/kill-fast culture, not the Builder program specifically). [Zineinc case study](https://zineinc.co.jp/case/dena流リーンスタートアップ。「3年間で24事業立ち上/)
- **Exit count / notable exits**: Not fully disclosed in public sources found; Delight Ventures materials reference multiple spin-outs but do not publish a consolidated IPO/M&A exit count. Unclear/not disclosed.
- **Success rate if disclosed**: Not disclosed for the Builder program. The internal case study above (24 launched / 19 closed / 3 years) implies a roughly 20% survival rate for DeNA's internal new-business pipeline, though this predates/is separate from Delight Ventures. [Zineinc](https://zineinc.co.jp/case/dena流リーンスタートアップ。「3年間で24事業立ち上/)
- **Notable failures**: Not specifically named in sources found; the "19 of 24 closed" statistic is the clearest public evidence of a high internal failure/kill rate. Unclear beyond that.
- **Profit/revenue structure**: Delight Ventures earns via traditional VC economics (equity stakes, fund management) plus DeNA provides resources/talent/domain expertise into spin-outs, taking equity as parent/investor. Not itemized publicly.
- **Competitive advantage/moat**: Access to DeNA's internal talent pool, decades of Japanese-internet-business operating know-how, DeNA balance sheet as anchor LP, and a formal "official career path to entrepreneurship" positioning ("起業をオフィシャルなキャリアパスに"). [FullSwing DeNA](https://fullswing.dena.com/archives/5782/), [Forbes JAPAN — Namba](https://forbesjapan.com/articles/detail/90997)
- **Hiring approach**: Sources both DeNA employees (via "Springboard" program for independent-minded staff) and external entrepreneurs (via "Venture Builder"/"V-Challe"/"V-ShIP"/"M-ShIP"), pairing them with DeNA product/AI experts in a "Domain Expert Program." [DeNA startup lab](https://www.denastartuplab.com/)
- **Org structure**: Delight Ventures operates as a legally independent VC (not a DeNA subsidiary) but with DeNA as primary/sole LP for the Builder funds, preserving "not a normal VC, not a CVC" positioning. [FullSwing DeNA (前編)](https://fullswing.dena.com/archives/5779/)
- **Investment criteria**: DVB3 fund (2025) prioritizes AI-centric "vertical industry transformation," "PEST-driven societal shift capture," and Japan-to-US cross-border entrepreneurship, targeting creation/seed-stage founders. [PR Times](https://prtimes.jp/main/html/rd/p/000000027.000052552.html)
- **AI usage in their process**: The 2025 DVB3 fund is explicitly AI-thesis-driven (AI as the "axis" of investment thesis) rather than AI being used operationally inside the studio process itself; also DeNA separately created "DeNA AI Link Co., Ltd." for AI consulting/enterprise AI agent business. [DeNA AI Link news](https://dena.com/intl/news/4753/); Namba on AI: "AI is not a bubble" — Toyo Keizai interview [東洋経済](https://toyokeizai.net/articles/-/926049)

## 2. CyberAgent — CyberAgent Capital / Internal Startup Spinoffs

- **Overview / model type**: Two distinct mechanisms: (1) **CyberAgent Capital** (formerly CyberAgent Ventures), the group's CVC/VC arm investing in internet/tech startups across Japan, Vietnam, Indonesia, Thailand, and the US; (2) internal subsidiary-creation ("子会社化") model where CyberAgent spins out in-house teams as legally separate subsidiaries (e.g., games, media) rather than a formal "startup studio" brand. [CyberAgent Capital](https://www.cyberagentcapital.com/), [CyberAgent history](https://www.cyberagent.co.jp/en/corporate/history/)
- **History**: Investment/incubation business launched Oct 2005 (CA-I investment partnership); CyberAgent Capital established 2006, now operating from 5 countries (Tokyo/Shibuya, Ho Chi Minh City, Jakarta, Bangkok, San Francisco). [FIRST CVC](https://www.firstcvc.jp/en/story/cyberagentcapital), [CyberAgent Capital](https://www.cyberagentcapital.com/)
- **Founders/leaders**: Group founder Susumu Fujita (CyberAgent CEO); CyberAgent Capital run as a dedicated investment subsidiary (specific current GP names not confirmed in sources found — unclear).
- **Funding raised**: CA Startups Internet Fund 4 (CASIF4) newly formed (exact size unclear/not disclosed in sources found); as of March 2021, CyberAgent Capital had invested across 8 countries/10 bases and supported 350+ companies. [Startup DB / CAC news](https://www.cyberagentcapital.com/news_event/casif4/)
- **Companies/businesses launched (internal spinoffs)**: Notable internally-incubated subsidiaries include Cygames, Sumzap, Applibot, QualiArts, Colorful Palette (games); AbemaTV (JV with TV Asahi); WINTICKET; Ameba (media). [CyberAgent History EN](https://www.cyberagent.co.jp/en/corporate/history/)
- **Exit count / notable exits**: CyberAgent itself is TSE-listed (4751); Cygames and other subsidiaries remain wholly/majority owned rather than independently IPO'd (i.e., CyberAgent's model favors keeping winners in-house rather than spinning out for external IPO). No clear public list of CyberAgent Capital portfolio IPOs found in this pass — unclear/not disclosed.
- **Success rate if disclosed**: Not disclosed.
- **Notable failures**: Not identified in sources found.
- **Profit/revenue structure**: CyberAgent's FY reporting shows internet advertising as core revenue, games (Cygames-led) and Media & IP (ABEMA, WINTICKET, Ameba) as other segments; ABEMA reportedly reached profitability alongside record group profits in recent reporting. [note.com IR analysis](https://note.com/yama_shukatsu/n/n1229362a8263?hl=en)
- **Competitive advantage/moat**: Deep media/ad-tech distribution (Ameba, ABEMA) to bootstrap new consumer products; internal capital markets; multi-country CVC network for early access to Southeast Asian deal flow.
- **Hiring approach**: Internal — new ventures typically staffed by existing CyberAgent employees promoted to found subsidiary companies (e.g., games studios), rather than external "founder-in-residence" recruiting typical of Western venture studios.
- **Org structure**: Parent-subsidiary "群経営" (group management) structure — many independently branded subsidiaries under the CyberAgent holding umbrella; CyberAgent Capital operates as a separate CVC/VC subsidiary investing externally.
- **Investment criteria**: CyberAgent Capital focuses on internet/mobile business models, seed-to-early stage, with hands-on growth support from pre-launch stage. [CyberAgent Capital](https://www.cyberagentcapital.com/)
- **AI usage in their process**: Not specifically documented in sources found for CyberAgent Capital's investment process; unclear/not disclosed.

## 3. Sun* Inc. (Sun Asterisk) — Startup Studio / Dev-Shop Hybrid (Vietnam/Japan)

- **Overview / model type**: TSE-listed (4053) "Digital Creative Studio" combining software-development outsourcing (engineering from Vietnam/Philippines/Cambodia) with startup co-creation/incubation ("Sun* Startup Studio Vietnam"). Two service lines: "Creative & Engineering" and "Talent Platform." [Sun* About](https://en.sun-asterisk.com/about/), [Shared Research profile](https://sharedresearch.jp/en/companies/4053)
- **History**: Founded 2012; now 1,500+ members across 6 cities in 4 countries (Vietnam, Japan, Philippines, Cambodia). IPO on Tokyo Stock Exchange Mothers, **31 July 2020**, offering price ¥700, first trade ¥1,209. [Sun* About](https://en.sun-asterisk.com/about/), [IPO data](https://minkabu.jp/stock/4053/ipo)
- **Founders/leaders**: Not individually confirmed in this pass (Vietnamese/Japanese co-founding team) — unclear/not disclosed from sources reviewed.
- **Funding raised**: Public company; FY2024 consolidated sales reported as ¥135.68bn* and parent net profit ¥10.23bn — **note: figures should be independently re-verified**, as automated summary output for this query showed unit inconsistency; market cap as of early April 2026 ≈¥18.17bn with ~40.03M shares outstanding. [Nikkei co. page](https://www.nikkei.com/nkd/company/?scode=4053), [Yahoo Finance JP performance](https://finance.yahoo.co.jp/quote/4053.T/performance)
- **Companies/businesses launched**: "Sun* Startup Studio Vietnam" positioned as a "factory for creating IT startup companies" combining capital, technology, and market access; exact count of startups created not disclosed in sources found. [Sun* Startups VN](https://startup.sun-asterisk.vn/)
- **Exit count / notable exits**: Sun* itself is the highest-profile "exit" (its own 2020 IPO); portfolio-company exit count not disclosed.
- **Success rate if disclosed**: Not disclosed.
- **Notable failures**: Not identified in sources found.
- **Profit/revenue structure**: Primarily fee-for-service software development/consulting revenue (dev-shop model) plus equity stakes in co-created startups — a hybrid "services fund the studio" model distinct from pure equity-only studios.
- **Competitive advantage/moat**: Low-cost, high-quality Vietnam-based engineering talent pool combined with Japan market access/business development — a cost-arbitrage + go-to-market bundle.
- **Hiring approach**: Large-scale engineering hiring in Vietnam/Philippines/Cambodia; recruiting-as-a-service ("Talent Platform") is itself a monetized business line.
- **Org structure**: Public holding company with in-house studio division; direct academic/innovation-center ties (e.g., consortium role at Hanoi University of Science and Technology SoICT). [SoICT](https://soict.hust.edu.vn/innovation/consortium/sun-inc.html)
- **Investment criteria**: Not specifically disclosed; studio model implies willingness to take equity in exchange for dev resources.
- **AI usage in their process**: Not specifically documented in sources found — unclear/not disclosed.

## 4. XTech Corporation / XTech Ventures / XTech Studio

- **Overview / model type**: Founded by former CyberAgent COO **Shinichi Saijo (西條晋一)** in 2018 as two linked entities — **XTech Corp** (which runs "XTech Startup Studio," pairing experienced new-business builders to launch multiple discontinuous startups) and **XTech Ventures** (a VC fund providing capital + startup know-how, initially targeting mid-career professional entrepreneurs). [XTech Startup Studio](https://xtech-corp.co.jp/), [XTech Ventures](https://www.xtech-ventures.co.jp/)
- **History**: Founded 2018. Saijo previously: Itochu → CyberAgent (COO/EVP from 2008) → co-founder/GP at WiL (2013–2017) → CEO of Qrio (2014). XTech Ventures has raised three funds to date: Fund 1 (~¥5.2bn), Fund 2 (~¥12.3bn, closed with plans to invest in 40+ companies), Fund 3 (~¥13bn, formed from Dec 2024, already deployed to 15 startups per early reporting, focused on energy, space/defense, and advanced tech alongside traditional software/consumer). *(Note: figures reported in units of "億円"; re-verify exact totals against XTech Ventures' official releases before quoting precisely, as automated extraction had unit-conversion risk.)* [XTech Ventures Fund2 release](https://www.xtech-ventures.co.jp/info/110), [Diamond Signal](https://signal.diamond.jp/articles/-/586), [PR Times Fund3](https://prtimes.jp/main/html/rd/p/000000029.000099021.html)
- **Founders/leaders**: Shinichi Saijo (西條晋一) — CEO/founder of both XTech Corp and XTech Ventures. [Wikipedia (JA)](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E8%A5%BF%E6%A2%9D%E6%99%8B%E4%B8%80), [XTech team page](https://www.xtech-ventures.co.jp/ourteam/saijo)
- **Funding raised**: See fund sizes above; cumulative AUM across 3 funds roughly in the ¥30bn class per aggregate reporting (needs verification).
- **Companies/businesses launched**: Portfolio list published at [xtech-ventures.co.jp/portfolio](https://www.xtech-ventures.co.jp/portfolio); XTech Startup Studio model explicitly aims to launch startups "continuously and non-contiguously" (連続的かつ非連続的に) rather than one-at-a-time.
- **Exit count / notable exits**: Funds 1 and 2 are reported to have already produced multiple IPOs, M&As, and secondary/stock-transfer exits, but an exact count was not found in this pass — unclear/not disclosed precisely.
- **Success rate if disclosed**: Not disclosed.
- **Notable failures**: Not identified in sources found.
- **Profit/revenue structure**: Standard VC fund economics (management fee + carry) at XTech Ventures; XTech Corp studio likely takes founder-level equity in launched companies.
- **Competitive advantage/moat**: Saijo's personal network and operating credibility (CyberAgent COO, WiL co-founder, Qrio CEO) attracts "mid-career" (i.e., experienced, not first-time 20-something) founder candidates — a differentiated founder-recruiting niche vs. typical youth-oriented Japanese seed VC.
- **Hiring approach**: Targets experienced mid-career operators/executives as studio "co-founders," explicitly marketed as a viable path for people who assume it's "too late" to found a startup mid-career. [STARTUP DB Media interview](https://lp.startup-db.com/media/articles/xtechventures-saijo)
- **Org structure**: Two-entity structure — XTech Corp (studio/operating company) + XTech Ventures (fund management company) — allowing the studio to both build companies directly and co-invest via the VC vehicle.
- **Investment criteria**: Fund 3 explicitly broadens beyond software/consumer into energy, space/defense, advanced technology — signaling a strategic tilt toward deep-tech/hard-tech alongside traditional SaaS. [PR Times](https://prtimes.jp/main/html/rd/p/000000029.000099021.000099021)
- **AI usage in their process**: Not specifically documented; unclear/not disclosed.

## 5 & 6. Relic Inc. and quantum Inc. — Corporate Venture Studios (Tokyo)

Note: the brief groups "Quantum" under Relic's "business design division," but research shows these are **two separate, unrelated companies**: (a) **Relic Inc.**, an independent Tokyo "business co-creation company" running the "ZERO1000 Ventures" startup studio; and (b) **quantum Inc.**, a Hakuhodo-group-affiliated startup studio. Reported separately below. (There is also a third, unrelated "Quantum Leap Corp." founded by ex-Sony chairman Nobuyuki Idei — noted for disambiguation only, not a core research target.)

### 5a. Relic Inc.

- **Overview / model type**: Self-described "事業共創カンパニー" (business co-creation company) with three pillars: incubation-tech SaaS products, new-business production/support services (consulting-style), and open innovation (startup investment + JVs with large enterprises). Runs docomo STARTUP program with NTT docomo. [Relic corporate site](https://relic.co.jp/), [Relic services](https://relic.co.jp/services/)
- **History**: Cumulative track record cited as 3,000+ client companies and 15,000+ new-business development engagements (company-wide, across all service lines, not just the studio). "Relic Startup Studio: ZERO1000 Ventures" launched January 2023 as a "free, all-directional" (無料・全方位型) studio that starts multiple startups simultaneously/consecutively. [PR Times launch](https://prtimes.jp/main/html/rd/p/000000140.000016318.html), [Biz/Zine](https://bizzine.jp/article/detail/8662)
- **Founders/leaders**: Not confirmed by name in this pass for current CEO — unclear/not disclosed (site: relic.co.jp/company).
- **Funding raised**: Relic is intentionally **not listed/IPO'd** as a matter of stated strategy (positions itself as an operating business-co-creation company rather than a fund-raising VC-style entity). [Search synthesis of relic.co.jp/company/about_data/]
- **Companies/businesses launched**: Via ZERO1000 Ventures (since Jan 2023): named outputs include RUFU (product distribution), MEAL FORWARD (foodtech), 3rd Economy (Web3×fintech), Pro3 Lab (outcome-based engineering services). A student-focused variant "ZERO1000 Ventures for student" was later launched and was adopted as a coordinating body by the Tokyo Metropolitan Government. [PR Times](https://prtimes.jp/main/html/rd/p/000000140.000016318.html)
- **Exit count / notable exits**: None of Relic's own studio spin-outs identified as IPO'd; Relic states it has supported many client/portfolio companies that later achieved IPOs through its broader consulting/investment work (not studio-specific). Unclear precise count.
- **Success rate if disclosed**: Not disclosed.
- **Notable failures**: Not identified.
- **Profit/revenue structure**: ZERO1000 Ventures participants are salaried employees of the Relic Group during validation (studio absorbs downside risk), with up to ¥10 million in validation expense provided per venture, in exchange for eventual founder-equity/spin-out terms — a classic "studio funds validation, takes founder equity" model.
- **Competitive advantage/moat**: Massive volume of new-business consulting engagements (3,000+ companies) gives Relic pattern-recognition/playbook depth and corporate-JV deal flow that a stand-alone studio would lack; docomo partnership gives access to telco distribution/corporate innovation budgets.
- **Hiring approach**: Recruits aspiring founders (including students via the dedicated program) into salaried "studio residency" roles with guaranteed pay during idea validation.
- **Org structure**: "Relic Holdings" appears in some releases (relic-holdings.co.jp), suggesting a holding-company restructure; ZERO1000 Ventures operates as a named internal program/brand rather than a separate legal entity.
- **Investment criteria**: Emphasizes broad/omnidirectional ("全方位型") intake rather than narrow vertical focus; process includes mindset/domain training, ideation, and prototype-based validation.
- **AI usage in their process**: In August 2025, Relic and DELTA Inc. formed a joint venture **"AI Rich Studio"** dedicated to AI business, indicating a distinct/newer AI-specific studio vehicle separate from ZERO1000 Ventures. [Relic press release](https://relic.co.jp/press-release/60335/)

- **Founders/leaders**: Not confirmed by individual name in this pass — unclear/not disclosed (positioned as "employees themselves become entrepreneurs" model per Hakuhodo internal profile).
- **Funding raised**: Not disclosed publicly as a fund/AUM figure (quantum appears to operate more as a project-fee + equity/hands-on studio rather than a traditional fund vehicle). Unclear/not disclosed.
- **Companies/businesses launched**: 100+ companies/universities engaged in incubation to date; approximately 40 projects running concurrently per year, many combining hardware+software new-business support for large corporates and startups. [Hakuhodo quantum feature](https://www.hakuhodo.co.jp/magazine/97892/)
- **Exit count / notable exits**: Not disclosed in sources found.
- **Success rate if disclosed**: Not disclosed.
- **Notable failures**: Not identified.
- **Profit/revenue structure**: Design/consulting project fees (large corporate clients) blended with equity/hands-on stakes in ventures created; design work has won iF Design, Red Dot, D&AD, Cannes Lions, Good Design Award recognitions, and pieces are in the permanent collections of Pinakothek der Moderne (Munich) and the Danish Design Museum — suggesting design-led IP/brand as a differentiator and possible revenue driver (licensing/consulting premium). [Hakuhodo quantum feature](https://www.hakuhodo.co.jp/magazine/97892/)
- **Competitive advantage/moat**: Backed by Hakuhodo's advertising/brand/creative resources and large-corporate client relationships — a "creativity-first" studio positioning distinct from finance-first VC studios; access to corporate innovation budgets via Hakuhodo's ad-agency relationships.

- **Org structure**: Operates as a subsidiary within the Hakuhodo group (formal legal name: 株式会社quantum), with a dedicated design studio sub-brand (MEDUM).
- **Investment criteria**: Not explicitly published; project selection appears client/corporate-partnership driven (~40 concurrent projects/year) as much as pure venture selection.
- **AI usage in their process**: Not specifically documented in sources found — unclear/not disclosed.

This is a notable market-entry gap/opportunity signal for a new entrant.

## Japan Market Context

### Startup funding environment 2024–2026
- Total Japan startup fundraising (equity, excl. debt): **2024: ¥779.3bn**; **2025: ¥761.3bn** (roughly flat YoY); number of fundraising companies fell ~6% to ~2,700 in 2025; median deal size fell from ¥77.6M (2024) to ¥62.4M (2025), indicating a "flight to quality"/selectivity trend rather than market collapse. [INITIAL/Speeda Japan Startup Finance 2025](https://initial.inc/articles/japan-startup-finance-2025), [Speeda Japan Startup Finance 2024 summary](https://jp.ub-speeda.com/news/20250121/)
- H1 2025 fundraising: **¥339.9bn**, up ~4% YoY. [Speeda H1 2025](https://initial.inc/articles/japan-startup-finance-2025h1)
- By funder type (2025): pure VC investment declined 8% to ¥297.3bn; **corporate (CVC) investment rose to ¥148bn** (+¥32bn); financial-institution investment rose to ¥36.7bn (from ¥31bn) — signaling **growing corporate/CVC appetite relative to traditional VC**, directly relevant to a studio model seeking corporate JV/LP partners. [JETRO/Chambers Japan VC 2025 trends](https://practiceguides.chambers.com/practice-guides/venture-capital-2025/japan/trends-and-developments)
- Series A funding fell from ¥168.4bn (2024) to ¥139.1bn (2025) with deal count down (519→501); Series B rose in total yen (¥172.6bn→¥192.4bn) but deal count fell (351→312) — capital concentrating into fewer, larger later-stage-of-early deals. [Speeda/journal.startup-db.com data](https://journal.startup-db.com/articles/funding-ranking-202412)
- **J-Startup program** (METI): selects high-potential startups via VC/corporate-nominator panel for concentrated public+private support; latest cohort announced March 2025. [METI J-Startup announcement](https://www.meti.go.jp/english/press/2025/0313_001.html), [J-Startup program](https://www.j-startup.go.jp/en/about/)
- **Government "Startup Development Five-Year Plan" targets for FY2027**: grow to 100,000 startups, nurture 100 unicorns, attract ¥10 trillion in cumulative startup investment — an aggressive national target that implies continued/expanding subsidy, tax, and regulatory support through the back half of the 2020s. [Japan Times, Dec 2025](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/2025/12/25/special-supplements/japans-startup-ecosystem-gains-global-attention/)
- JIC (Japan Investment Corporation), a government-linked fund-of-funds, remains an active LP/policy vehicle in this ecosystem. [JIC Dec 2025 presentation](https://www.j-ic.co.jp/en/news/.assets/e_20251217_JIC_Presentation2.pdf)

### Labor market factors relevant to a studio model
- **IT/digital talent shortage**: METI estimates a shortfall of up to **790,000 IT workers by 2030**, with an already-existing shortfall of roughly **430,000 as of 2025** — a structural constraint any studio's hiring/founder-recruiting plan must account for. [MHLW/METI labor research](https://www.mhlw.go.jp/content/11600000/001244078.pdf)
- **Side-jobs/secondary employment (副業・兼業)**: A national policy push exists to legitimize corporate employees taking on side projects/second jobs (relevant to "founder-in-residence while still employed" studio models); a 2018-era matching program between big-company talent and regional SMEs produced 109 matches, 66 of them via side-job/secondary-employment arrangements — illustrating this channel is real but still small-scale. [METI/Mirasapo case data](https://mirasapo-plus.go.jp/hint/20187/)
- **Foreign tech talent / visas**: Foreign nationals working under the "Engineer/Specialist in Humanities/International Services" (技術・人文知識・国際業務) visa status reached **418,706 people at end of 2024**, up 56,360 YoY — a sharp increase indicating growing reliance on and availability of foreign engineering talent. Note: reporting also flags **stricter visa-screening procedures from April 2026** for this category, which could tighten future foreign-hiring flexibility. [Immigration Services Agency data synthesis / kedomo visa guide](https://career.kedomo.com/engineer/)
- **Lifetime-employment culture shift**: Broader context (from search synthesis, not independently re-verified with a dedicated primary source in this pass) indicates continued gradual erosion of traditional lifetime employment norms among younger/urban professionals, with DeNA's "Springboard"/"official career path to entrepreneurship" framing (Section 1) being a direct corporate response/case study of this shift. [FullSwing DeNA](https://fullswing.dena.com/archives/5782/)

### Regulatory considerations for AI companies in Japan
- **AI事業者ガイドライン (AI Business Operator Guidelines)**: Jointly issued by METI and the Ministry of Internal Affairs and Communications (総務省). **v1.0 released April 19, 2024** as a non-binding "soft law" framework; **v1.1 released March 28, 2025**; **v1.2 released March 31, 2026** — an actively iterating "living document," not a fixed statute. Targets three actor classes: AI developers, AI providers, and AI users (i.e., covers the full stack from model-builders to business deployers). Developed via multi-stakeholder process (academia, civil society, industry) rather than unilaterally by government. [METI v1.0 press release](https://www.meti.go.jp/press/2024/04/20240419004/20240419004.html), [METI v1.1 PDF](https://www.meti.go.jp/shingikai/mono_info_service/ai_shakai_jisso/pdf/20250328_2.pdf), [METI v1.2 PDF (2026)](https://www.meti.go.jp/shingikai/mono_info_service/ai_shakai_jisso/pdf/20260331_1.pdf)
- **Practical implication for a studio**: Japan currently favors **soft-law/guideline-based AI governance** (as opposed to the EU's hard-law AI Act model) — lower near-term compliance burden for an AI studio launching multiple ventures quickly, though this could tighten as the guideline matures through future versions. [Deloitte Japan AI governance blog series](https://www.deloitte.com/jp/ja/services/audit-assurance/blogs/ai-governance-07.html)

### Unique advantages Japan offers an AI startup studio
- **Corporate JV/CVC appetite is rising, not falling**: 2025 data shows corporate investment growing (+¥32bn YoY) even as pure VC investment contracted (-8%) — Japan's large corporates are increasingly willing LPs/JV partners for a studio model, a structural tailwind distinct from more VC-dominated markets. [JETRO/Chambers 2025](https://practiceguides.chambers.com/practice-guides/venture-capital-2025/japan/trends-and-developments)
- **Government subsidy/compute support specifically for generative AI**: GENIAC provides free/subsidized cloud compute (reported ¥8.4bn allocation for one tranche via Google Cloud), dataset support, and structured matching with user companies and VCs — directly lowering the capital cost of standing up multiple AI ventures inside a studio. [METI Journal GENIAC feature](https://journal.meti.go.jp/p/38757/)
- **Robotics/hardware ecosystem**: Major Japanese corporates (Toyota, KDDI, NEC, Fujitsu, Mitsubishi Electric, GMO Internet Group) are formal members of the **AI Robot Association (AIRoA)**, launched March 2025 to build shared data infrastructure for robotics — a hard-tech/physical-AI ecosystem with corporate co-investment appetite that most Western AI-studio markets lack at this depth. [Nikkei xTECH](https://xtech.nikkei.com/atcl/nxt/column/18/03118/00001/)
- **Talent-cost and demographic factors**: Structural IT-talent shortage (see Labor section) is double-edged — it raises domestic engineering costs but also increases corporate willingness to fund/partner with external studios/CVCs to access innovation capacity they cannot build in-house, and increases openness to foreign engineering talent under eased-then-tightening visa regimes.
- **Existing playbook density**: Japan already has multiple functioning studio/company-builder precedents (Delight Ventures/DeNA, Relic/ZERO1000, quantum/Hakuhodo, XTech) that a new AI-focused entrant can benchmark, partner with, or differentiate against, rather than needing to educate the market on the studio model from zero. (Sections 1–9 above.)
