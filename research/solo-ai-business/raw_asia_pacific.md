# Raw research batch: Asia-Pacific (China/Korea/Singapore/India/Australia/Taiwan/HK) solo AI business cases (unedited agent output)

Agent note (kept verbatim — important methodological finding): Extensive research across IndieHackers, X/Twitter-linked interviews, Reddit/HN-adjacent aggregators, KrAsia/TechInAsia/e27/YourStory/Inc42, Chinese press (36Kr, Sina Finance, ChinaVenture, 21jingji, SCMP), Korean and Taiwanese sources, and startup revenue databases (GetLatka, Tracxn) turned up very few cases that satisfy all three filters simultaneously: (1) founder/company based in China, South Korea, Singapore, India, Australia, Taiwan, or Hong Kong, (2) team of 0-3 people, and (3) $1M+ ARR/revenue or clearly extreme margins, with a verifiable source. Most of the famous "solo founder hits $1M" stories (Pieter Levels, Danny Postma, Marc Lou, Tony Dinh, Damon Chen) are Western nationals based in Vietnam, Thailand, Bali, or Portugal — excluded even though they surfaced repeatedly. Most India/China AI ventures disclosing $1M+ revenue (Yellow.ai, Julius AI, Locofy, Orbo.ai, Disha AI, Creati/Perceptual Leap, PicWish) are VC-funded with 10-200+ employees, disqualifying them on team size.

### Heptabase
- Founder: Alan Chan (Yu-An Chan), with co-founders Yeefun Lin and Ethan Wu
- Country: Taiwan (Taipei; incorporated via Stripe Atlas)
- Product: AI-enhanced visual note-taking / "second brain" whiteboard app for learning/knowledge management
- Customers: Students, PhD researchers, knowledge workers doing deep learning/writing
- Revenue/ARR: $1.2M ARR reported for 2024 (GetLatka); Starter Story cites ~$200K/month (~$2.4M annualized). Press-reported.
- Profit margin: not disclosed (raised ~$1.7M YC-led seed 2022 — not purely bootstrapped)
- Employees: Started 3 co-founders; grew to ~8 by 2026 — exceeds 0-3 at current scale, included with caveat
- AI usage: AI-generated summaries/Q&A embedded in visual note cards ("ask AI" against your own whiteboard KB)
- Customer acquisition channel: X build-in-public, YouTube demos, Product Hunt, YC network, PKM community word of mouth
- Source URLs: https://getlatka.com/companies/heptabase.com ; https://www.starterstory.com/heptabase-breakdown ; https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/heptabase

### SiteGPT
- Founder: Bhanu Teja P, with co-founder Dheeraj
- Country: India (IIT Madras grad)
- Product: No-code AI chatbot builder trained on a business's own docs/website
- Customers: SMBs and SaaS companies needing automated customer support
- Revenue/ARR: Progression: $250K rev year 1 solo → ~$300K ARR mid-2024 → $18K MRR (late 2025) → $28K MRR early 2026 (~$336K annualized) → $58.3K combined monthly across his AI tools March 2026 (~$700K annualized run-rate). Short of strict $1M bar but high-margin, compounding. Self-reported + press-reported (Superframeworks, Metaschool, NexusBlog, SQ Magazine, IdeaIndex).
- Profit margin: not precisely disclosed, typical 70-90%+ gross margin category; prior product Feather sold outright for $250K
- Employees: 2 (Bhanu + Dheeraj) plus a self-described AI ops agent ("Jarvis")
- AI usage: Core product is LLM-powered chatbot; also uses AI agents internally for support/marketing
- Customer acquisition channel: Building in public on X, Product Hunt, SEO/content, carried-over audience from Feather exit
- Source URLs: https://superframeworks.com/blog/sitegpt ; https://www.nexusblog.in/2026/06/from-village-in-india-to-28kmonth-bhanu.html ; https://metaschool.so/articles/making-250k-year-with-ai

### 一舟一课 (Li Yizhou AI Courses) — FLAGGED: compliance/regulatory risk case
- Founder: Li Yizhou (李一舟)
- Country: China
- Product: Paid AI-skills courses/info-products teaching consumers to use ChatGPT-style tools, sold via WeChat mini-program/Douyin livestreams
- Customers: Mass-market consumers/side-hustlers seeking "AI money-making" skills
- Revenue/ARR: RMB 175M (~$24M) cumulative over ~3 years per multiple Chinese outlets (36Kr, Sina Finance, ChinaVenture); ~250,000 units of a RMB199 course sold in one year (~RMB50M/~$7M). Press-reported.
- Employees: Not clearly disclosed; business registry shows 8 affiliated companies under his name — implies larger than 0-3 people, flagged as not a clean "micro business" fit
- AI usage: Sells AI-literacy/prompting education rather than building AI software
- CAVEAT: Feb 2024, Chinese regulators forced his WeChat mini-program offline and banned his video-channel account amid credential-fraud accusations, missing ICP licensing, and unattributed use of open-source models — a cautionary/compliance-risk case, not a clean success story
- Source URLs: https://www.36kr.com/p/3233572902108801 ; https://www.chinaventure.com.cn/news/78-20250403-385762 ; https://www.21jingji.com/article/20240226/herald/9ddcbe7f7b537c0bd54f5e185f95163a.html

## Coverage gap (important finding for MarketAnalysis.md)
No verifiable $1M+/0-3-person case was found for South Korea, Singapore, Australia, or Hong Kong despite targeted searches. Companies found there were either well below the $1M bar (e.g., "one-person company" trend pieces citing $70K-$280K/year) or VC-funded with double-digit headcounts (Yellow.ai, Wrtn, Notta, Locofy, PopAi). This mirrors the Japan finding: outside the US/Western-Europe/nomad-founder cluster, verifiable solo/micro $1M+ AI cases are still rare in 2025-2026 — a genuine white-space signal, not just a research-coverage artifact.
